The fifth game in the series is Forza Horizon 5

Top

Forza Horizon 5 is a 2021 racing video game developed by Playground Games and published by Xbox Game Studios. It is the fifth Forza Horizon title and twelfth main instalment in the Forza series. The game is set in a fictionalised representation of Mexico. It was released on 9 November 2021 for Windows, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S. The game received critical acclaim and became a commercial success upon release; it launched to over ten million players in the first week, the biggest-ever launch for an Xbox Game Studios game. The game won IGN's Game of the Year award and three jury-voted awards at The Game Awards 2021, tying with Hazelight's It Takes Two for most wins.

Gameplay

The game is also set in a fictional representation of Mexico, featuring the largest map in the series, 50% larger than its predecessor, Forza Horizon 4. The map includes diverse features such as an active caldera volcano, jungles, beaches, ancient Mayan temples, and towns like Guanajuato. The game introduces a new local weather system that affects the eleven unique biomes on the map differently.

For the first time the game introduces a new Horizon Arcade with mini-multiplayer games, an EventLab for creating custom games and races, and a feature called “Forza Link”, an AI assistant that helps players connect and play together online. Accolades, a new feature, allows players to collect points and prizes for completing tasks.

The Eliminator battle royale game mode from Forza Horizon 4 returns, with players starting in a 1963 Volkswagen Beetle. Players can buy in-game houses to unlock rewards and the game features over 800 licensed vehicles. Due to licensing issues, vehicles from Alfa Romeo, Fiat, and Lancia were not present in the base game, but they returned to the Forza series in the Series 24 update of the game on 17 August 2023.

Content and gameplay updates

Forza Horizon 5 has seen numerous conte nt updates and bug fixes since its release, with monthly updates introducing new cars, collectibles, horns, clothing, and more. These updates often have a specific theme and include series-specific map changes. For example, the Series 2 update in December 2021 was Christmas-themed, and the Series 5 update introduced a stunt park in the stadium on the map. In March 2022, the game added American and British Sign language interpreter videos for cinematics. The Series 13 update in October 2022 celebrated the tenth anniversary of the Forza Horizon series, adding new content including a new radio station, a new Horizon Story, the return of Midnight Battles, and new achievements, accolades, and badges.

The Series 14 update in November 2022 added a new Horizon Story featuring Donut Media. After a money-making exploit was discovered in the game’s Super7 mode, the game’s auction house was disabled and a patch was released to fix the exploit. In June 2023, a 1956 Chvrolet Corvette and 2022 GMC Hummer EV pickup were added to the game as part of a collaboration with Xbox Game Studios and Mattel.

Expansions

The game's first expansion, Forza Horizon 5: Hot Wheels, a follow-up to a similar expansion for Forza Horizon 3, was released on 19 July 2022. This announcement was accidentally leaked via a Steam listing three days before its official announcement. The game's second expansion, Forza Horizon 5: Rally Adventure, was released on 29 March 2023. It includes a new map, a variety of off-road vehicles such as trophy trucks and buggies, and a new career mode. A car pack expansion of the game titled Forza Horizon 5: Fast X – Car Pack was featured on 5 December 2023, featuring the vehicles from the 2023 film Fast X, the tenth installment of the Fast & Furious franchise and the second of the series since Forza Horizon 2 in 2015.
